Frequently Asked Questions

1. What are the requirements a Program participant or mentee should fulfill during the semester?

a. Attend one orientation;

b. Complete a Mentee Improvement Plan (also available under Resources in this app) prior to meeting a mentor;

c. Meet or communicate with mentor once a week (Communications via Facebook, Twitter, email, texting and phone are all acceptable);
d. Attend three workshops (Academic or Life Skills workshops);

e. Participate in one community service activity on-campus or off-campus for at least two hours before the end of the semester. Click here to see a list of volunteer opportunities with Stockton's Community Partners.

Note: An email or a letter from the supervisor who supervised the mentee's community service will suffice as proof of completion and should be submitted via email to:care@stockton.edu. 

2. What is a Life Skills Workshop?

A Life Skills Workshop is a workshop designed to help students responsibly manage their personal affairs. Some examples are: Time Management, Managing Stress and How to Deal with Test Anxiety.

3. What is an Academic Workshop?

An Academic Workshop is a workshop designed to help students learn more about their selected courses, majors or academic pursuits. Some examples are: Communication Studies Workshop, Pre-Physical Therapy Workshop, How to Prepare for Advising, Understanding the Financial Aid Process and Hospitality Management Workshop.

4. How many workshops should a mentee attend each semester?

 A mentee should attend at least three workshops (Life Skills or Academic Workshops) and is allowed to attend workshops offered by other Stockton departments.

5. Can a mentee withdraw or leave the Program?

Yes, if the mentee joined the Program voluntarily. On the other hand, if a mentee was required by the Office of Financial Aid to join the Program because the student has to fulfill the College's Financial Aid Satisfactory Academic Progress Policy, then this particular mentee cannot leave the Program unless approved by the Office of Financial Aid.

6. What will happen if a mentee who is the second category mentioned above leaves the Program anyway?

The student could lose his or her financial aid or status as a college student at Stockton.
