FAQ’s for Personal and Peer Mentors

1. When will I get information about my mentee?

A mentor will be provided with information of his or her new mentee before the start of classes.

2. How will my mentee know that I am his/her mentor?

A mentee will receive a letter from the CARE Program with instructions on how to contact his or her mentor.

3. As Peer Mentor, how should I prepare myself in my first meeting with my mentee?

Review the Peer Mentor Manual (p. 8-12) and the Mentee Improvement Plan (p. 34).

4. Should I ask my mentee of his or her Mentee Improvement Plan (MIP) during the first meeting?

Yes, and review all information entered by your mentee in the MIP.

5. What should I do if my mentee has not yet completed his or her Mentee Improvement Plan by the first meeting?

Ask your mentee if he or she needs help in completing his or her MIP. MIPs should be completed by the third weekly mentor-mentee meeting.

6. Is a mentee required to answer all questions in the MIP?

It depends on their situation. For example, a mentee maybe working full–time and is taking 4 courses. Therefore, it is understandable if a mentee decides not to join any student organization and not complete the Student Involvement Plan section of the MIP. Other sections of the MIP, however, should be completed.

5. How will mentees know what is required from them?

Mentees will be provided with a copy of the MIP during their orientation and will be instructed to complete their MIP prior to meeting their mentors.

6. What other requirements a mentee should fulfill during the semester?

a. Attend one orientation;
b. Meet with mentor once a week ( Communications via Facebook, Twitter, email, texting and phone are all acceptable);
c. Attend three workshops (Academic or Life Skills workshops).

d. Participate in one community service activity for at least two hours before the end of the semester.  Click here to see a list of volunteer opportunities with Stockton's Community Partners.

Note: An email or a letter from the supervisor who supervised the mentee's community service will suffice as proof of completion and should be submitted via email to:care@stockton.edu.

7. What is a Life Skills Workshop?

A Life Skills Workshop is a workshop designed to help students responsibly manage their personal affairs. Some examples are: Time Management, Managing Stress and How to Deal with Test Anxiety.

8. What is an Academic Workshop?

An Academic Workshop is a workshop designed to help students learn more about their selected courses, majors or academic pursuits. Some examples are: Communication Studies Workshop, Pre-Physical Theraphy Workshop, How to Prepare for Advising, Understanding the Financial Aid Process and Hospitality Management Workshop.

9. How many workshops should a mentee attend each semester?

A mentee should attend at least three workshops (Life Skills or Academic Workshops) and is allowed to attend workshops offered by other Stockton departments.

10. What will happen if a mentee has not completed the Program requirements at the end of the semester?

The CARE Program Coordinator will review all information pertaining to the mentee. The review may result in providing additional support and focus to the mentee or withdrawal of the mentee from the Program.

11. Can a Personal or Peer Mentor continue to be a mentor in the CARE Program each semester?

Yes, as long as the Peer Mentor is in good academic standing (at least a cumulative GPA of 2.75) and perform his or her responsibilities as depicted on page 4 of the 2013 Peer Mentor Manual.

 As for Personal Mentors (faculty and staff), they may continue as mentors and may leave the Program as long as they advise the CARE Team prior to leaving the Program.

12. Can a mentee withdraw or leave the Program?

Yes, if the mentee joined the Program voluntarily. On the other hand, if a mentee was required by the Office of Financial Aid to join the Program because the student has to fulfill the College's Satisfactory Academic Progress Policy then this particular mentee cannot leave the Program unless approved by the Office of Financial Aid.

13. What will happen if a mentee who is the second category mentioned above leaves the Program anyway?

The student could lose his or her financial aid or status as a college student at Stockton.
